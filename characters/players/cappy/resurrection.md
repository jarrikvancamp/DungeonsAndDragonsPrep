## The Death

Cappy fell during the climactic battle against "The Snake" (Nezznar) in Wave Echo Cave. Her brave sacrifice protecting her companions left the party devastated, but her death created ripples in the magical weave that attracted unwanted attention.

## The Necromancer's Interest

**Valindra Shadowmantle**, a former member of the Emerald Enclave turned rogue necromancer, has been studying the connection between life, death, and the natural world. She detected the unique magical signature of Cappy's death—a Moon Druid's passing creates distinct disturbances in the flow of natural energy.

Valindra sees an opportunity to test her theories about the intersection of druidic magic and necromancy, particularly in relation to the ancient powers connected to the Shattered Obelisk.

## The Dark Pact: The Bone Mother

Valindra serves as a conduit for **The Bone Mother**, an ancient goddess of death and corrupted nature who dwells in the deepest reaches of the Shadowfell. This malevolent deity claims dominion over the perversion of natural cycles, feeding on the corruption of life and the animation of death.

### The Bone Mother's Offer

The entity appears to Cappy's spirit in visions as a gaunt, towering figure wreathed in decaying vegetation and crowned with bleached antlers. Her skeletal face is framed by writhing vines that bear thorns dripping with poisonous sap, and her hollow eye sockets burn with sickly green flames.

*"Little druid, your precious cycle has been broken. Death has claimed you before your time, but I offer a different path. Return to flesh, but know that you shall be forever marked by my touch. Your nature magic will carry the taint of decay, your Wild Shape the whisper of bone. In exchange, you shall serve as my herald in the realm of the living. Will you accept my corruption and rise again?"*

## New Ability: Necrotic Convergence

Cappy gains a powerful new ability that manifests invisibly, channeling the Bone Mother's dark power through her natural magic. There are no visible signs when activated—the corruption flows silently through her druidic abilities.

```yaml
Necrotic Convergence:
  Uses: Proficiency bonus per long rest (3/long rest at level 5)
  Activation: Bonus action
  Duration: 1 minute
  
  Effect: Choose one of the following for the duration:
    - Free Wild Shape uses (does not count against daily limit)
    - Healing spells restore maximum possible hit points (no rolling)
  
  Mark of the Bone Mother: Each use requires a d20 roll:
    - On 1-3: Gain one level of Necrotic Corruption
```

## The Mysterious Cost: Necrotic Corruption

Each time the Mark of the Bone Mother triggers, Cappy gains a level of "Necrotic Corruption"—the goddess's influence gradually corrupting her natural essence.

### Necrotic Corruption Levels

**Level 1 - Subtle Decay:**
- Eyes occasionally flicker with pale green flames
- Touch causes flowers to wilt slightly
- Small animals instinctively avoid her presence
- *No mechanical effects*

**Level 2 - Physical Corruption:**
- Patches of pale, cold skin with dark veins visible beneath
- Hair develops streaks of ash gray
- Wild Shape forms show signs of decay (exposed bone, withered patches)
- *Advantage on Intimidation checks against living creatures*

**Level 3 - Death's Touch:**
- Healing spells cause nearby plants to blacken and die
- Food spoils faster in her presence
- Whispers from the Bone Mother demanding darker acts
- *Can sense the presence of Shattered Obelisk fragments within 1 mile*

**Level 4 - Necrotic Resonance:**
- Presence causes milk to sour, metal to tarnish, wood to rot
- Undead creatures regard her with curious interest rather than hostility
- Vivid nightmares showing locations of ancient burial sites and dark magic
- The Bone Mother's voice becomes clearer, more insistent
- *Once per long rest, can cast Speak with Dead without expending a spell slot*

**Level 5 - The Bone Mother's Possession:**
- **Immediate Effect:** The Bone Mother takes direct control of Cappy's body for 1d4 hours
- **During Possession:** 
  - Cappy's player loses control of the character
  - The DM controls Cappy as an NPC with the Bone Mother's personality
  - Cappy speaks in the Bone Mother's voice and pursues dark objectives
  - May attempt to corrupt others, seek out sources of necromantic power, or perform dark rituals
  - Physical appearance becomes gaunt and skeletal during possession
- **After Possession:**
  - Cappy has no memory of what happened during the possession
  - Corruption level resets to 0, but the experience leaves permanent marks
  - Gains permanent resistance to necrotic damage, vulnerability to radiant damage
  - Other characters may have witnessed disturbing behavior

### Reducing Corruption

Corruption levels can be reduced through:
- **Purification rituals** at blessed natural sites (holy springs, consecrated groves)
- **Atonement** through significant acts of healing and restoration
- **Cleansing** fragments of the Shattered Obelisk with divine magic
- **Divine intervention** from powerful clerics serving life deities
- **Genuine sacrifice** to protect innocent life

**Note:** After experiencing Level 5 possession, the corruption cycle can begin again, but each subsequent possession becomes longer and more dangerous (1d4+1 hours, then 1d4+2 hours, etc.).

## Story Hooks & Consequences

### Immediate Effects
- Cappy returns with faint dark veins visible beneath pale skin
- She has no memory of her time "away" but occasionally speaks of decay and bone
- Her connection to nature feels wrong—corrupted, tainted with death

### Ongoing Plot Threads
1. **Valindra's Research:** The necromancer will periodically seek to study Cappy's corruption
2. **Bone Mother's Agenda:** Dark visions leading to sites of ancient evil and buried secrets
3. **Druid Circle Reaction:** Other druids immediately sense the wrongness within her
4. **Obelisk Connection:** Growing awareness that the corruption feeds on the campaign's central mystery

### Potential Allies & Enemies
- **Emerald Enclave:** Will likely try to cleanse or exile her
- **Other Marked:** Cultists and undead who serve the Bone Mother
- **Religious Orders:** Clerics who view her as an abomination to be destroyed
- **The Party:** How do they react to their companion's dark transformation?

## DM Notes

- This resurrection creates direct ties to the Shattered Obelisk storyline through dark corruption
- **Level 5 Possession provides dramatic roleplay opportunities and story tension**
- The possession mechanic allows the DM to advance dark plots while giving the player consequences
- Valindra can serve as recurring NPC studying the intersection of death magic and nature
- The Bone Mother's agenda can drive the party toward darker plot developments through possessions
- Multiple paths exist for redemption or deeper corruption depending on player choices
- **Possession Duration:** Each subsequent possession becomes longer and more dangerous
- **Party Dynamics:** Other players must decide how to handle their possessed companion

### Running Possession Scenes
- The Bone Mother has knowledge Cappy doesn't possess
- She may reveal information about the Shattered Obelisk or other campaign secrets
- Use the possession to advance plots, create tension, or introduce new threats
- The possessed Cappy should feel alien and wrong to other characters
- Consider having the Bone Mother attempt to recruit other party members
