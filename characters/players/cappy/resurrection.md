## The Death

Cappy fell during the climactic battle against "The Snake" (Nezznar) in Wave Echo Cave. Her brave sacrifice protecting her companions left the party devastated, but her death created ripples in the magical weave that attracted unwanted attention.

## The Necromancer's Interest

**Valindra Shadowmantle**, a former member of the Emerald Enclave turned rogue necromancer, has been studying the connection between life, death, and the natural world. She detected the unique magical signature of Cappy's death—a Moon Druid's passing creates distinct disturbances in the flow of natural energy.

Valindra sees an opportunity to test her theories about the intersection of druidic magic and necromancy, particularly in relation to the ancient powers connected to the Shattered Obelisk.

## The Dark Pact: The Twilight Sovereign

Valindra serves as a conduit for **The Twilight Sovereign**, an ancient entity existing between the Feywild and Shadowfell. This forgotten being claims dominion over the eternal cycles of life, death, and rebirth.

### The Sovereign's Offer

The entity appears to Cappy's spirit in visions as a towering figure with humanoid upper body and lower body of twisting roots and shadows. Its antlered crown bears the phases of the moon, with stars glittering across its skin.

*"Child of the old ways, your journey need not end here. The cycles turn eternally, and death is merely transformation. I offer you return to your form, empowered by my twilight blessing. In exchange, you shall carry a fragment of my essence, helping it grow through the natural cycles you cherish. Will you accept my mark and return to your companions?"*

## New Ability: Twilight Convergence

Cappy gains a powerful new ability manifesting as a shadowy aura with star-flecked darkness surrounding her when activated. Her eyes turn completely black with pinpoints of starlight.

```yaml
Twilight Convergence:
  Uses: Proficiency bonus per long rest (3/long rest at level 5)
  Activation: Bonus action
  Duration: 1 minute
  
  Effect: Choose one of the following for the duration:
    - Advantage on Wisdom ability checks
    - Advantage on Wild Shape-related ability checks or attack rolls
    - Advantage on saving throws to maintain concentration
    - Roll healing dice twice and take the higher result
  
  Mark of the Sovereign: Each use requires a d20 roll:
    - On 1-3: Gain one level of Twilight Exposure
```

## The Mysterious Cost: Twilight Exposure

Each time the Mark of the Sovereign triggers, Cappy gains a level of "Twilight Exposure"—the Sovereign's influence gradually manifesting in her physical form.

### Twilight Exposure Levels

**Level 1 - Subtle Signs:**
- Eyes occasionally flash with starlight
- Shadow moves independently for brief moments
- Plants around her bloom or wither unpredictably
- *No mechanical effects*

**Level 2 - Physical Manifestation:**
- Small patches of bark-like skin with bioluminescent patterns
- Hair takes on hints of deep blue and purple
- Wild Shape forms gain constellation-like markings
- *Advantage on Intimidation checks against creatures of the natural world*

**Level 3 - Energy Imbalance:**
- Healing spells cause nearby plants to wither slightly
- Damage to enemies causes nearby vegetation to flourish briefly
- Occasional whispers from the Twilight Sovereign with cryptic instructions
- *Can sense the presence of Shattered Obelisk fragments within 1 mile*

**Level 4 - Twilight Resonance:**
- Presence causes minor disruptions: water ripples, flames lean toward her
- Animals grow unnaturally still or agitated around her
- Vivid dreams showing locations of obelisk fragments
- *Once per long rest, can cast Speak with Dead without expending a spell slot*

**Level 5 - Sovereign's Vessel:**
- Physical transformation becomes pronounced
- During full moons, Wild Shape extends to more exotic forms (DM discretion)
- The Sovereign may occasionally take control to observe or interact
- *Resistance to necrotic damage, vulnerability to radiant damage*

### Reducing Exposure

Exposure levels can be reduced through:
- **Purification rituals** at ancient natural sites (moonlit pools, sacred groves)
- **Offerings** to traditional elven or nature deities
- **Cleansing** fragments of the Shattered Obelisk
- **Divine intervention** from powerful clerics or paladins

## Story Hooks & Consequences

### Immediate Effects
- Cappy returns with faint geometric patterns occasionally glowing beneath her skin
- She has no memory of her time "away" but occasionally mentions things she shouldn't know
- Her connection to nature feels different—more primal, more dangerous

### Ongoing Plot Threads
1. **Valindra's Research:** The necromancer will periodically seek "samples" from Cappy
2. **Sovereign's Agenda:** Visions leading to significant campaign locations
3. **Druid Circle Reaction:** Other druids sense the unnatural force within her
4. **Obelisk Connection:** Growing awareness of the campaign's central mystery

### Potential Allies & Enemies
- **Emerald Enclave:** May try to help or ostracize her
- **Other Marked:** Servants of the Twilight Sovereign who recognize her
- **Religious Orders:** Clerics who view her as blessed or cursed
- **The Party:** How do they react to their changed companion?

## DM Notes

- This resurrection creates direct ties to the Shattered Obelisk storyline
- The Twilight Exposure system provides both benefits and consequences
- Valindra can serve as recurring NPC (ally, enemy, or neutral researcher)
- The Sovereign's agenda can drive plot developments
- Multiple paths exist for character development and story resolution

*The price of returning from death is never simple, and Cappy's bargain with the Twilight Sovereign will shape not only her future, but potentially the fate of the entire campaign.*
