# Chapter 1: A Dangerous Journey

## Background
More than five hundred years ago, clans of dwarves and gnomes made an agreement known as the Phandelver Pact, by which they would share a rich mine in a wondrous cavern known as Wave Echo Cave. In addition to its mineral wealth, the mine contained great magical power. Human spellcasters allied themselves with dwarves and gnomes to channel and bind that energy into a great forge called the Forge of Spells, where magic items could be crafted.

Times were good, and the nearby town of Phandalin prospered as well. But disaster struck when bandits swept through the North and laid waste to all in their path. A powerful bandit force reinforced by evil mercenary wizards attacked Wave Echo Cave to seize its riches and magic treasures. Human wizards fought alongside their dwarf and gnome allies to defend the Forge of Spells, and the ensuing spell battle destroyed much of the cavern. Few survived the cave-ins and tremors, and knowledge of the location of Wave Echo Cave was lost.

Recently, a trio of dwarves—the Rockseeker brothers—discovered the entrance to Wave Echo Cave, and they intend to reopen the mines. Unfortunately, a mysterious villain known as the Spider controls a network of bandit gangs and goblin bands in the area, and his agents followed the Rockseekers to their prize.

## Running This Chapter

### Character Advancement
- Characters should be 1st level when the chapter begins
- Characters gain a level when they finish exploring the Cragmaw hideout

### Setup Tasks
Before getting into the adventure's events:

1. **Character Introductions**: Encourage players to introduce their characters to each other
2. **Connection to Gundren**: Ask players how their characters know Gundren Rockseeker if using the "Meet Me in Phandalin" hook
3. **Transportation Details**: Determine who's driving the wagon and how characters are traveling

## The Road to Phandalin

### DM Read-Aloud
> You began your adventuring career in the city of Neverwinter. A dwarf named Gundren Rockseeker hired you to bring a wagonload of provisions to the rough-and-tumble settlement of Phandalin, a couple of days' travel south of the city. Gundren was clearly excited and more than a little secretive about his reasons for the trip, saying only that he and his brothers had found "something big," and that he'd pay you ten gold pieces each for escorting his supplies safely to Barthen's Provisions, a trading post in Phandalin. He then set out ahead of you on horse, along with a warrior escort named Sildar Hallwinter, claiming he needed to arrive early to "take care of business."
>
> You've spent the last few days following the High Road south from Neverwinter, and you've just recently veered east along the Triboar Trail. You've had no trouble so far, but you know this territory can be dangerous. Bandits and outlaws have been known to lurk along this road.

### Driving the Wagon
- Any character can drive a wagon (no skill required)
- Two oxen pull the wagon
- If no one holds the reins, the oxen stop
- **Cargo**: Mining supplies and food worth 100 gp total

## Goblin Ambush

### DM Read-Aloud
> You've been on the Triboar Trail for about half a day and are nearing a side road leading south toward Phandalin. As you come around a bend, you stumble upon the scene of a recent battle. The woods press close to the trail here, with a steep embankment and dense thickets on either side. Two horses wander the road, sniffing at ransacked personal effects.

### Scene Details
- Site littered with arrows, torn fabric, and discarded items
- Horses belong to Gundren Rockseeker and Sildar Hallwinter
- Empty leather map case lies nearby
- **Enemies**: 4 goblins hiding in woods (2 per side)

### Closer Inspection
> The horses' saddlebags have been looted. An empty leather map case lies nearby.

### Combat
- Goblins wait until someone approaches the horses
- Fight to the death until one goblin remains; that one flees toward the trail

### Development
- Characters might capture goblins for information
- Characters might miss the trail and continue to Phandalin (skip to Chapter 2)
- If wagon is captured, characters can buy gear in Phandalin and return

## What the Cragmaws Know
Captured goblins can be persuaded to reveal:

- **Bugbear Leader**: Klarg leads them, reports to King Grol at Cragmaw Castle (20 miles northeast)
- **Capturing Gundren**: The Spider paid Cragmaws to capture Gundren and send him to King Grol
- **Sildar's Location**: Human companion held in "eating cave" (area H6), ~15 goblins in hideout
- **Strange Goblins**: Recent newcomers with elongated skulls and glowing green weapons; Cragmaws fear them

## Goblin Trail
- Trail found on north side of road (DC 10 Survival to read signs)
- 5 miles northwest to Cragmaw hideout
- Two traps along the way

### Snare (10 minutes in)
- **Detection**: DC 15 Perception if searching for traps
- **Trigger**: DC 10 Dex save or restrained 10 feet up
- **Escape**: 1 slashing damage to cord; careful lowering or 1d6 fall damage

### Pit Trap (20 minutes in)
- **Detection**: DC 15 Perception 
- **Trigger**: DC 10 Dex save or fall 10 feet for 1d6 damage
- Easy to climb out (walls not steep)

## Cragmaw Hideout

### General Features
- Cave slopes steeply upward inside a hill
- **Ceilings**: 20-30 feet high with stalactites
- **Light**: H1-H2 outside, rest dark unless noted
- **Sound**: Water muffles noise (DC 15 Perception between areas)
- **Stream**: 2 feet deep, slow-moving
- **Stalagmites**: Provide cover

### Locations

#### H1: Cave Mouth
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> The goblins' trail ends at a cave in a hillside five miles from the scene of the ambush. A shallow stream flows from the cave mouth, which is screened by dense briar thickets. A narrow, dry path leads into the cave on the right side of the stream.

- Stream 2 feet deep, cold and slow
- Thicket in H2 impenetrable from west side
- **Development**: Noise alerts goblins in H2

#### H2: Goblin Guard Post
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> On the east side of the stream that flows from the cave mouth, a small area in the briar thickets has been hollowed out to form a lookout post. Wooden planks flatten the briars and provide room for guards to lie hidden and watch the area—including several goblins lurking there right now!

**Enemies**: 2 goblins, 1 goblin boss
- Thickets provide 3/4 cover
- Can surprise with Stealth vs. their Perception
- **Treasure**: Goblin boss has 16 sp
- **Development**: When boss defeated, remaining goblins flee to H3

#### H3: Kennel
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> Just inside the cave mouth, a few uneven stone steps ascend to a small, dank chamber on the east side of the passage. The cave narrows to a steep fissure at the far end and is filled with the stench of animals. Three chained wolves snarl and rattle their chains as you approach the cave mouth. Each wolf's chain leads to an iron rod driven into the base of a stalagmite.

**Enemies**: 3 wolves (chained)
- Wolves can't reach targets on steps
- **Animal Handling**: DC 15 (DC 10 with food) to calm
- **Fissure**: Chimney to H8 (DC 10 Athletics to climb)
- **Development**: Goblins can release wolves; wolves can break free with successive DC 15/10 Strength checks

#### H4: Steep Passage
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> The main passage from the cave mouth climbs steeply upward, the stream plunging and splashing down its west side. In the shadows, a side passage leads west across the other side of the stream.

**With light/darkvision**:
> In the shadows of the ceiling to the north, you can just make out the dim shape of a rope bridge crossing over the passage ahead of you. Another passage 20 feet above the floor appears to intersect this one.

- **Goblin Bridge Guard**: DC 12+ Perception spots
- **Western Passage**: Giant poisonous snake on unstable ledge
- Snake attacks climbers; unstable ledge triggers collapse (DC 10 Dex save)

#### H5: Overpass  
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> The stream continues beyond another set of uneven steps ahead, bending eastward. A waterfall burbles in a larger cavern somewhere ahead of you.

**Enemies**: 1 goblin (well-concealed, DC 15 Perception)
- **Rope Bridge**: 20 feet above stream (DC 15 Athletics to climb walls)
- Bridge: AC 5, 10 hp, vulnerable to fire/slashing
- **Flood Warning**: If alerted, signals H7 to release flood

**Flood Mechanics**:
- Goblins in H7 knock away dam supports
- Next round: water surges from H7 to H1
- **Saves**: DC 10 Dex to avoid sweep, then DC 15 Str to hold on
- **Failure**: Washed to H1, 2d6 damage, prone

#### H6: Goblin Den
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> This large cave is divided in half by a ten-foot-high escarpment. A steep, natural staircase leads from the lower portion to the upper ledge. The air is hazy with the smoke of a cooking fire, and pungent from the smell of poorly cured hides and unwashed goblins.

**Enemies**: 5 goblins, 2 goblin bosses (Yeemik, Errk)
- **Approach**: DC 14 Stealth or spotted
- **Sildar Hallwinter**: Prisoner on upper ledge, 1 hp

**Yeemik's Bargain**:
- If losing, grabs Sildar: "Truce, or this human dies!"
- Wants party to kill Klarg in H8, bring back head
- Sildar warns not to trust goblin
- If refused, shoves Sildar off ledge (1d6 damage)

**Sildar's Information**:
- Lords' Alliance agent, investigating missing wizard Iarno Albrek
- Rockseeker brothers found Wave Echo Cave entrance
- "The Spider" ordered Gundren's capture
- Strange goblin whispered "you're not what Ruxithid wants"

**Treasure**: 
- Errk: 21 cp
- Yeemik: 3 agates (10 gp each), potion of healing

#### H7: Twin Pools Cave
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> This cavern is half-filled with two large pools of water. A narrow waterfall high in the eastern wall feeds the pool, which drains out the western end of the chamber to form the stream that flows from the cave mouth below. Low fieldstone walls serve as dams holding the water in. A wide exit stands to the south, while two smaller passages lead west. The sound of the waterfall echoes through the cavern, making it difficult to hear.

**Enemies**: 3 goblins, 2 wolves
- **Rock Dams**: Control water flow for flood trap
- One goblin flees to warn Klarg if fighting starts
- Areas H7 and H8 can't hear each other due to waterfall

#### H8: Klarg's Cave
**DM Read-Aloud**:
> Sacks and crates of looted provisions are piled in the south end of this large cave. To the west, the floor slopes toward a narrow opening that descends into darkness. A larger opening leads north down a set of natural stone steps, and the roar of falling water echoes from beyond. In the middle of the cavern, the coals of a large fire smolder.

**Enemies**: Klarg (bugbear), 2 goblins, 1 wolf (Ripper)
- **Fire Pit**: 1 fire damage to enter, 1d6 if prone
- **Natural Chimney**: Shaft down to H3
- **Supplies**: Provide half cover, marked with blue lion (Lionshield Coster)

**Klarg's Personality**:
- Delusions of grandeur, refers to self in third person
- "Who dares defy Klarg?" and "Klarg will build a throne from your bones!"

**Development**:
- If warned: Klarg and allies hide for ambush (passive Perception 14+ avoids surprise)
- If surprised: Can surprise by climbing from H3
- If alone: Attempts to flee down chimney (DC 10 Dex or restrained)

**Treasure**:
- **Stolen Supplies**: Need wagon to transport, 50 gp reward from Lionshield Coster
- **Treasure Chest**: 1,700 cp, 150 sp, 2 potions of healing, jade frog statuette (40 gp)

## What's Next?
Characters should head to Phandalin for several reasons:
- **Deliver Supplies**: Complete Gundren's job at Barthen's Provisions
- **Escort Sildar**: Wounded warrior needs safe passage
- **Seek NPCs**: Background connections or other hooks

*Alternative*: If players seek Cragmaw Castle immediately, skip to Chapter 3.
