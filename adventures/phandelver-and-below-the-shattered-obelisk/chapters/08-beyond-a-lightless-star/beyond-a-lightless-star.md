## Chapter Overview

The mind flayer fanatics have fled to the Far Realm, to the bizarre heart of their godlet Ilvaash's domain. There, they commune directly with their wicked deity as they begin the sinister ritual that will transform Humanoids in and around Phandalin into mind flayers. The characters must navigate the Briny Maze, overcome its dangers, reach the Endless Void, and stop the fanatics before their vile plan comes to fruition.

## Quick Reference

**Key Locations:**
- Phandalin (if characters return - showing corruption)
- The Briny Maze (Ilvaash's brain-like domain)
- The Endless Void (nodules floating in psychic space)
- Ilvaash's Anima (final confrontation site)

**Major NPCs:**
- The Three Fanatics (Chishinix, Hashutu, Voalsh)
- Rescued Townspeople (across multiple nodules)
- Refraction of Ilvaash (final boss encounter)

**Primary Objectives:**
- Navigate the Briny Maze's organic passages
- Explore floating nodules in the Endless Void
- Rescue remaining Phandalin prisoners
- Confront the mind flayer fanatics
- Stop the transformation ritual
- Defeat Ilvaash's refraction

## Character Advancement

- **Starting Level:** 10th level
- **Level 11:** After reaching the entrance to the Endless Void
- **Level 12:** After vanquishing all three mind flayer fanatics

## In the Far Realm

**Exhaustion Risk:** Any non-Far Realm creature finishing a long rest in the Far Realm must make DC 15 Wisdom save or gain 1 level of exhaustion.

## Doom Comes to Phandalin (Optional Return)

If characters return to Phandalin before pursuing the fanatics, they find the town in the grip of accelerating transformation:

### Creeping Transformations
- **Buildings:** Appear oddly askew, warping in space
- **Natural Elements:** Shadows undulate, especially in moonlight
- **Townspeople:** Many babble in Deep Speech about "the coming ascendance"
- **Physical Changes:** Eyes in strange places, aberrant mutations

### Key NPC Changes
- **Gwyn Oresong:** Double-irised eye on neck, reluctant to socialize
- **Rivibiddel:** Misreading his precious text as chaos-glorifying
- **Stonehill Inn:** Closed due to safety concerns

## The Briny Maze

**Type:** Ilvaash's Far Realm domain shaped like a titanic brain
**Layout:** Organic tunnels leading to central Briny Pool

### Briny Maze Features

- **Spongy Construction:** Pinkish-gray, wrinkled, self-healing material
- **Unpleasantly Moist:** Thin sheen of spoiled-smelling moisture
- **Illumination:** Eerie violet light throughout (bright light)
- **Ceilings:** 16 feet high where folds meet
- **Mindgates:** Three special doors requiring activation rod pairs

### Mindgate System

**Activation Method:** Create living chain between charged rods
**DC 15 Constitution Save:** 22 (4d10) lightning damage (half on success)
**Disadvantage:** If wearing metal armor

**Mindgate Locations:**
- **Rear Mindgate (B13-B14):** Opened by rods in B7 or B22
- **Central Mindgate (B14-B19):** Opened by rods in B17 or B22
- **Forward Mindgate (B18-B22):** Opened by rods in B21 or B22

### Lobe Benefits (Intelligence 3+ creatures)

- **Occipital Lobe (B1-B5):** Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks
- **Temporal Lobe (B6, B10):** Enhanced auditory effects
- **Parietal Lobe (B7-B9, B11-B13):** Can't have prone condition unless chosen
- **Frontal Lobe (B14-B22):** Can Help as bonus action

## Major Briny Maze Encounters

### B3: Webbed Chamber
- **Enemies:** 2 phase spiders (can't use Ethereal Jaunt), 2 psionic ashenwights
- **Environment:** Diaphanous webs (lightly obscure, heavily obscure near back wall)
- **Tactics:** Phase spiders alert ashenwights, coordinate attacks

### B4: Wall of Hubris
- **Enemies:** 5 flameskulls (emerge from wizard corpses)
- **Background:** Wizards attempted to steal Ilvaash's secrets, failed and died
- **Tactics:** Flameskulls pursue fleers and fight until destroyed

### B7: Githyanki Camp
- **NPCs:** Varakkta (githyanki knight), Kianka and Vazzi (warriors)
- **Missing Item:** Varakkta's silver greatsword (lost in B5)
- **Information:** Maze layout, brain monster location, mind flayer sightings
- **Potential Allies:** If treated fairly, may share intelligence

### B9: Shalfi's Digging Spot
- **NPCs:** Shalfi Lewin (aberrant zealot) and Jitterjaws (flesh meld)
- **Activity:** Digging hole to reach area B17 below
- **Roleplay:** Obsessed with catching "the dragon" (Lowarnizel)
- **Combat:** Both fight to death if threatened

### B10: Left Temporal Lobe
- **NPC:** Jomlus (spectator) reading Truths of the Inward Facing Mind
- **Benefit:** 24-hour assistance grants Blessing of the Solipsistic Mind
- **Function:** Universal translation of all communication in this area

### B14: Prospective Lair
- **Enemy:** Mublinesh (beholder) seeking to earn Ilvaash's approval
- **Goal:** Kill 100 intruders to gain lair (at 96/100)
- **Tactics:** Retreats to B15 if reduced below 50 hit points

### B17: Dragon's Den
- **NPCs:** Lowarnizel (young amethyst dragon) and Gossa (human mage)
- **Alliance:** Cautiously welcoming to non-Ilvaash forces
- **Information:** Activation rods, Endless Void details, fanatics' route
- **Base:** Offers secure rest location

### B21: Ashripask's Alcove
- **NPC:** Ashripask (arcanaloth) abandoned by her bodyguards
- **Experiments:** Using rods to sense creatures throughout maze
- **Information:** Details about denizens, maze mechanics
- **Treasure:** Mindblasting cap

### B22: Briny Pool - Entrance to Endless Void
- **Challenge:** 6-mile deep pool filled with breathable cerebral fluid
- **Enemy:** Purple worm with swimming speed and tremorsense
- **Navigation:** DC 14 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Stealth) each mile
- **Advantage:** If viewed pool through Golcuus's eyepiece in Chapter 7
- **Exit:** Pinprick of darkness at bottom leads to Endless Void

## The Endless Void

**Type:** Vast emptiness with floating nodules (islands)
**Movement:** Flying speed equal to walking speed, can hover
**Illumination:** Swirling colored light (bright throughout)
**Visibility:** Limited to 500 feet due to hazy effects

### Endless Void Features

- **Air:** Breathable but smells like scorched metal
- **Gravity:** Each nodule has 100-foot gravity field
- **Intellect Snares:** Random encounters (1d6 per hour if aimlessly floating)

### Nodule Travel System

Roll twice on d8 table for nearby visible nodules (reroll duplicates):

| d8 | Nodule |
|----|---------|
| 1  | Crystal Dome |
| 2  | Empty Bridge |
| 3  | Mire of Doubt |
| 4  | The Nematode |
| 5  | Occluding Miasma |
| 6  | Toppled Statue |
| 7  | Wailing Battlefield |
| 8  | Ilvaash's Anima (only after 5+ other nodules visited) |

## Key Nodule Encounters

### Crystal Dome (Starting Location)
- **Enemies:** 5 shield guardians (moved inside by fanatics)
- **Prisoners:** 4 Phandalin townspeople (Thawnia, Shansa, Wramble, Unmiel)
- **Status:** Glued to dome interior with sticky mucus
- **Information:** Mind flayers took 5 others to "big fallen statue" island

### Empty Bridge
- **Enemies:** Aboleth and water elemental in dark river
- **Trap:** Symbol spell on bridge (DC 19, hopelessness + blindness)
- **NPC:** Dead wizard (headless, skull exploded from psychic overload)
- **Treasure:** Amulet of proof against detection, valuable souvenirs

### Mire of Doubt
- **Enemies:** 2 spirit nagas (Valsyx and Charnyz)
- **Environment:** Swamp with difficult terrain off winding path
- **Tactics:** Nagas coordinate, delay with riddles/challenges
- **Rejuvenation:** Return 1d6 days after death

### The Nematode (Living Nodule)
- **Type:** Massive brain parasite functioning as terrain
- **Entry:** Mouth closes when creatures approach throat structure
- **Enemies:** 2 intellect snares in throat
- **Challenge:** Must release brain fluid pressure to open mouth
- **Danger:** Destroying membrane entirely causes acid regurgitation

### Occluding Miasma
- **Environment:** 200-foot diameter cloudkill effect (permanent)
- **Enemies:** 3 invisible stalkers guarding metal tongue
- **Hidden Item:** Metal tongue needed for Toppled Statue
- **Challenge:** Finding 25-foot center nodule in poisonous fog

### Toppled Statue
- **Prisoners:** 4 Phandalin townspeople in statue base (Hovnar, Menvin, Nalpina, Mildra)
- **Puzzle:** Need metal tongue from Occluding Miasma to open base
- **Information:** Mind flayers plan to use captives' brains for "new elder brain"
- **Condition:** Prisoners are starving and nearly overcome with despair

### Wailing Battlefield
- **Environment:** Ancient battlefield with perpetual wailing sounds
- **Enemies:** Mezzoloths led by Nellik (nycaloth) in windmill
- **Patrols:** 2 pairs of mezzoloths roam battlefield
- **Conflict:** Some mezzoloths disobeying Nellik's orders
- **Treasure:** Flayer slayer greataxe

## Ilvaash's Anima - Final Confrontation

**Access:** Only visible after visiting 5+ other nodules
**Environment:** Shallow pit surrounded by stone tentacles, mists showing miniature Phandalin

### The Three Fanatics

**Locations depend on obelisk fragments acquired:**

| Fragments | Chishinix Location | Hashutu Location | Voalsh Location |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| 4         | A1                | A2               | A4              |
| 5         | A1                | A4               | A4              |
| 6-7       | A4                | A4               | A4              |

**Stats:** Mind flayer clairvoyants with modifications:
- **Chishinix:** Accompanied by severed head (encephalon gemmule stats)
- **Hashutu:** Blindsight 60 feet
- **Voalsh:** Immune to prone, flying speed 30 feet

**Additional Forces:**
- **7 Fragments:** 2 mind flayer prophets join fanatics

### A4: Pit Overlook - Final Battle

**Environment:** Reassembled obelisk on dais, misty pit showing corrupted Phandalin buildings
**Sounds:** Cries of despair from townspeople

#### Obelisk Mechanics
- **Life Energy Link:** Fanatics tied their life force to obelisk
- **Backlash System:** Damage to obelisk transfers to nearest fanatic
- **Damage Multiplier:** 
  - If characters secured fragments in Ch. 6: 2x damage to fanatic
  - If characters secured no fragments: 1x damage to fanatic
- **Destruction:** Obelisk becomes dust when all fanatics die

#### Combat Tactics
- **Fanatics:** Fight to death, may struggle to avoid being nearest to obelisk
- **Positioning:** Use terrain and flying abilities
- **Desperation:** Target rescued townspeople to demoralize characters

## Ilvaash's Refraction - True Final Boss

**Trigger:** When characters return to Briny Pool (B22) after defeating fanatics
**Appearance:** Enormous floating ghostly brain with razor-sharp claws
**Additional Threat:** Purple worm joins fight on round 3 (if still alive)

**Combat Notes:**
- Fights until destroyed
- Targets rescued townspeople to create despair
- Represents only a sliver of Ilvaash's true power

## Wrapping Up

### Victory Consequences
- **Briny Maze:** Shudders and begins destabilizing
- **Far Realm Access:** Second gateway opens to deeper Far Realm
- **Sealed Illithinoch:** Original gateway closes after 1 hour
- **Phandalin Restored:** All corruption effects reversed

### Return to Phandalin
- **Transformation Reversal:** All aberrant changes undone
- **Hero's Welcome:** Characters hailed as saviors
- **Scholarly Interest:** Gwyn Oresong wants detailed accounts
- **Permanent Welcome:** Warm reception for life in Phandalin

## Combat Tactics by Environment

### Briny Maze Tactics
- **Terrain Use:** Leverage lobe benefits and narrow passages
- **Mindgate Control:** Control access between areas
- **Environmental Hazards:** Use unstable structures and organic weapons

### Endless Void Tactics
- **Three-Dimensional Combat:** Use flying movement creatively
- **Nodule Hopping:** Use gravity fields tactically
- **Visibility Limits:** 500-foot range affects spell targeting

### Final Confrontation Tactics
- **Obelisk Targeting:** Use backlash system strategically
- **Positioning Management:** Force fanatics near obelisk
- **Prisoner Protection:** Balance offense with rescue priorities

- **Boost Allies:** Stronger assistance from rescued NPCs

### Harder (Level 11-12 Party)
- **Enhanced Fanatics:** Add legendary actions
- **Additional Threats:** More encounters per nodule

## Potential Epilogue Hooks

### Attack of the Nematode
The massive creature awakens and comes to Phandalin seeking revenge, bringing intellect snares and requiring internal exploration.

### Far Realm Explorers
Characters explore deeper Far Realm using the opened gateway, facing new threats and discovering strange communities.

### Ilvaash's Revenge
The godlet sends brain breakers and other forces to hunt the characters throughout the Far Realm.

## What If They Fail?

If characters are defeated:
- **Incapacitated Condition:** Trapped in Briny Maze
- **Reinforcement Option:** Gwyn sends new heroes from Neverwinter
- **Rescue Mission:** New characters save previous party
- **Alternative Victory:** Later success still saves Phandalin

## DM Notes

### Managing the Far Realm
- **Maintain Atmosphere:** Emphasize alien, unsettling nature
- **Track Exhaustion:** Long rest penalties create urgency
- **Use Description:** Rich sensory details enhance immersion

### Nodule Exploration
- **Player Choice:** Allow characters to choose exploration order
- **Information Gathering:** Use rescued NPCs to provide crucial details
- **Time Management:** Balance thorough exploration with narrative pacing

### Final Confrontation
- **Epic Scale:** Emphasize the stakes and scope of the threat
- **Tactical Options:** Multiple approaches to defeating fanatics
- **Emotional Payoff:** Celebrate saving Phandalin and its people

This chapter provides a satisfying conclusion to the campaign while opening possibilities for future Far Realm adventures.
