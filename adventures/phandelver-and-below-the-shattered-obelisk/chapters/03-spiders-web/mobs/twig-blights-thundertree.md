# Twig Blights (Thundertree)

## Role
Animated plant creatures infesting the ruins of Thundertree

## Tactical Overview
**Combat Style**: Ambush predators using false appearance
**Preferred Range**: Melee with surprise attacks
**Key Abilities**: False Appearance, Multiple units, Vulnerability to fire

## Stats
```yaml
Twig Blight:
  AC: 13 (Natural Armor)
  HP: 4 (range 3-6)
  Speed: 20 ft
  STR: 6 DEX: 13 CON: 12 INT: 4 WIS: 8 CHA: 3
  
  Skills: Stealth +3
  Damage Vulnerabilities: Fire
  Damage Resistances: Bludgeoning, piercing
  Senses: Blindsight 60 ft (blind beyond), passive Perception 9
  Languages: Understands Common but doesn't speak
  Challenge: 1/8 (25 XP each)
  
  Special Abilities:
    Animating Spores: When destroyed, nearby plants may animate
    False Appearance: Indistinguishable from normal shrubs when motionless
  
  Actions:
    Claws: +3 to hit, 1d4+1 slashing damage
```

## Encounter Groups
**Small Group**: 2-3 twig blights (CR 1/4 total)
**Medium Pack**: 4-6 twig blights (CR 1 total) 
**Large Infestation**: 8-10 twig blights (CR 2 total)

## Location Distribution (Thundertree)

### Common Areas
- **U1 (Village Square)**: 2-3 blights hiding among overgrown plants
- **U3 (Blacksmith Shop)**: 1-2 blights in ivy-covered walls
- **U5 (Herbalist Shop)**: 3-4 blights among medicinal plants
- **U6 (General Store)**: 2 blights in collapsed roof vegetation

### Dense Concentrations
- **U9 (Druid's Cottage)**: 4-6 blights, more aggressive near druid's magic
- **U2 (Inn Ruins)**: 3-4 blights in common room overgrowth
- **Forest Perimeter**: Scattered individuals throughout approach paths

## Tactical Behavior
**Ambush Phase**: Remain motionless until prey comes within 10 feet
**Attack Pattern**: Coordinate attacks to surround single target
**Retreat Triggers**: Fire damage or area effects cause panic
**Pack Tactics**: Don't gain advantage but attack simultaneously

## Environmental Integration
**False Appearance**: Perception DC 15 to spot before they move
**Terrain Advantage**: Use thick vegetation for concealment
**Surprise Attacks**: Often get surprise round against unaware parties
**Natural Camouflage**: Blend seamlessly with Thundertree's overgrowth

## Animating Spores Mechanic
When a twig blight is destroyed:
- **Trigger**: Death releases spores in 10-foot radius
- **Effect**: 1-2 normal plants animate as new twig blights
- **Timing**: Occurs at start of next round
- **Duration**: New blights last for remainder of encounter only

## Fire Vulnerability Tactics
**Player Discovery**: First fire damage reveals vulnerability
**Panic Response**: All blights within 30 feet move away from fire sources
**Area Denial**: Fire spells/torches create safe zones
**Environmental Fire**: Can spread to surrounding vegetation

## Encounter Scaling
**Level 1-2 Party**: Use small groups (2-3 blights)
**Level 3-4 Party**: Use medium packs (4-6 blights) 
**Level 5+ Party**: Use large infestations with animating spores

## Stealth & Detection
**Stealth Bonus**: +3 to Stealth, advantage in plant-heavy areas
**Detection Methods**: 
- Passive Perception 15+ spots suspicious vegetation
- Investigation DC 12 reveals unnatural movement patterns
- Nature DC 10 identifies plants as wrong type for season

## Regional Effects
**Druid's Influence**: Reidoth can command these creatures (if encountered)
**Venomfang's Territory**: Dragon's presence makes them more aggressive
**Magical Corruption**: Some areas have enhanced blight spawning

## Alternative Outcomes
**Avoidance**: Careful parties can navigate around major concentrations
**Druid Intervention**: Reidoth can call them off if alliance formed
**Fire Clearing**: Systematic burning can clear areas permanently
**Environmental Solution**: Remove magical source to stop animation

## Treasure
**No Individual Hoard**: Blights don't collect items
**Environmental**: May have grown around/over previous victims' equipment
**Hidden Items**: Bodies of past victims concealed beneath vegetation
**Magical Components**: Destroyed blights yield 1d4 pieces of animated wood

## Roleplay Opportunities
**Druid Communication**: Reidoth can translate their simple desires
**Environmental Storytelling**: Reveal Thundertree's tragic history
**Fear Factor**: Build tension through rustling vegetation
**Problem Solving**: Encourage creative solutions beyond combat

## Development Hooks
- **Magical Source**: What causes the animation? Ancient druid magic?
- **Reidoth's Burden**: Druid feels responsible for their existence
- **Venomfang's Influence**: Does dragon magic affect plant growth?
- **Clearing Thundertree**: Permanent solution needed for settlement

## Scaling Options
**Easier**: Reduce numbers, remove animating spores ability
**Harder**: Add larger plant creatures, increase stealth bonuses
**Environmental**: Emphasize navigation and avoidance over combat
**Horror**: Use for atmospheric tension and jump scares
