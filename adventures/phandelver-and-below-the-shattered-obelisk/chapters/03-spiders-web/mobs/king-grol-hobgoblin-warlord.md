# King Grol (Hobgoblin Warlord)

## Role
King of the Cragmaw tribe and ruler of Cragmaw Castle

## Tactical Overview
**Combat Style**: Frontline commander with battlefield control
**Preferred Range**: Melee with reach weapon and intimidation
**Key Abilities**: Leadership, Multiattack, Shield Wall tactics

## Stats
```yaml
King Grol (Hobgoblin Warlord):
  AC: 20 (Plate, Shield)
  HP: 97 (range 80-115)
  Speed: 30 ft
  STR: 16 CON: 16 DEX: 14 INT: 14 WIS: 11 CHA: 15
  
  Saving Throws: Str +6, Dex +5, Wis +3
  Skills: Intimidation +5, Athletics +6
  Languages: Common, Goblin
  Challenge: 6 (2,300 XP)
  
  Special Abilities:
    Martial Advantage: Extra 2d6 damage when ally adjacent to target
    Leadership (Recharges 4-6): Ally within 30 ft adds d4 to attack or save
  
  Actions:
    Multiattack: Two longsword attacks or two javelin attacks
    Longsword: +9 to hit, 1d8+6 slashing (1d10+6 two-handed)
    Shield Bash: +9 to hit, 1d4+6 bludgeoning, target DC 14 Str save or prone
    Javelin: +8 to hit, range 30/120, 1d6+6 piercing
    Parry: +3 AC against one attack (reaction)
```

## Location: Cragmaw Castle (C8)

### Throne Room Features
- **Stone Throne**: Damaged but imposing centerpiece
- **High Ceiling**: 15-foot vaulted stone ceiling
- **Multiple Entrances**: Can call for reinforcements
- **Defensive Position**: Natural choke points and cover

### Initial Setup
- **Bodyguards**: 2 hobgoblin guards flanking throne
- **Prisoners**: May have captured NPCs as leverage
- **Alert Status**: Aware of party approach if alarm raised

## Personality & Leadership
- **Authoritative**: Commands absolute respect from tribe
- **Pragmatic**: Willing to negotiate from position of strength
- **Paranoid**: Suspects treachery, especially from Vyerith
- **Proud**: Hobgoblin military tradition and honor

## Combat Tactics
**Round 1**: Uses Leadership to boost ally attacks, positions defensively
**Round 2+**: Focuses most dangerous enemy, uses Shield Bash to prone
**Martial Advantage**: Coordinates with bodyguards for flanking
**Defensive Fighting**: Uses Parry reaction and high AC effectively

## Negotiation Potential
- **Prisoner Exchange**: May trade captives for valuable consideration
- **Information**: Knows about Black Spider operations and regional threats
- **Respect Strength**: More likely to deal with proven warriors
- **Honor Code**: Hobgoblin military traditions may require fair treatment

## Reinforcements
**Available Forces**:
- 2 Hobgoblin bodyguards (already present)
- 4 Goblins from adjacent areas (arrive round 3)
- 1 Owlbear from courtyard (arrive round 4, if still alive)

**Calling for Help**: Uses action to shout commands in Goblin

## Relationship with Vyerith
- **Tension**: Doesn't fully trust the doppelganger advisor
- **Dependency**: Relies on Vyerith's shapeshifting abilities
- **Suspicion**: May suspect Vyerith of ulterior motives
- **Alliance**: United against common threats but wary

- **Gundren's Fate**: Knows location and status of captured dwarf
- **Black Spider Intel**: Has information about Spider's operations
- **Regional Politics**: Aware of threats to tribal territories

## Environmental Factors
- **Stone Construction**: Good cover, difficult to damage
- **Multiple Levels**: Throne room has balcony access
- **Narrow Passages**: Limits movement, favors defenders
- **Castle Alarm**: Combat here alerts entire stronghold

## Alternative Outcomes
**Peaceful Resolution**: 
- Prisoner exchange for safe passage
- Information trade about mutual enemies
- Temporary alliance against Black Spider

**Combat Victory**:
- Access to castle vault and intelligence
- Eliminates major regional threat
- Potential to recruit surviving Cragmaws

**Retreat**:
- May regroup with remaining forces
- Could become recurring antagonist
- Might ally with other threats

## Roleplaying Notes
**Voice**: Deep, commanding, militaristic
**Mannerisms**: Sits rigidly upright, gestures with weapon
**Values**: Honor in battle, loyalty to tribe, military efficiency
**Fears**: Loss of control, tribe dissolution, stronger enemies

## Scaling Options
**Easier**: Remove one bodyguard, reduce HP to 80, delay reinforcements
**Harder**: Add magic weapon properties, increase bodyguard count
**Political**: Emphasize negotiation over combat
**Tactical**: Use more complex battlefield with multiple levels
