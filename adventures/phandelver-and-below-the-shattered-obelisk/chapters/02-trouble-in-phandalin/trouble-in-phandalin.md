# Chapter 2: Trouble in Phandalin

## Background
The frontier town of Phandalin is built on the ruins of a much older settlement. Hundreds of years ago, old Phandalin was a thriving town whose people were firmly allied with the dwarves and gnomes of the Phandelver Pact. However, the same bandits who sacked the mines at Wave Echo Cave attacked the settlement, and Phandalin was abandoned for centuries.

In the last three or four years, hardy folk from the cities of Neverwinter and Waterdeep have begun settling atop the ruins of Phandalin. A bustling frontier town has developed, home to farmers, woodcutters, fur traders, and prospectors. Unfortunately, more than a few ruffians and bandits have settled here as well. A gang known as the Redbrands has been hounding Phandalin, extorting and bullying everyone in town. The gang is led by a mysterious figure known to the townsfolk only as "Glasstaff."

## Running This Chapter

### Character Advancement
- Characters should be 2nd level when the chapter begins
- Characters gain a level when they finish exploring the Redbrand hideout under Tresendar Manor

### Chapter Goals
This chapter introduces important townspeople who become quest givers and friends throughout the adventure. Consider portraying the townsfolk as friendly and curious about the adventurers to encourage emotional ties for later chapters.

## Welcome to Phandalin

### DM Read-Aloud
> The rutted track emerges from a wooded hillside, and you catch your first glimpse of Phandalin. The town consists of forty or fifty simple log buildings, some built on old fieldstone foundations. More old ruins—crumbling stone walls covered in ivy and briars—surround the newer houses and shops, showing how this must have been a much larger town in centuries past. Most of the newer buildings are set on the sides of the cart track, which widens into a muddy main street as it climbs toward a ruined manor house on a hillside at the east side of town.
>
> As you approach, you see children playing on the town green, and townsfolk tending to chores or running errands at the shops in town. Many people look up and smile as you approach, but all return to their business as you go by.

**Proprietor**: Toblen Stonehill (friendly young human from Triboar)
- Former prospector who realized innkeeping was more profitable
- Dislikes Redbrands but fears retaliation
- **Rooms**: 5 of 6 available (Sildar occupies one)

#### Rumors at the Inn
- **Elsa** (dwarf bartender): Daran Edermath is a former adventurer; sister Gwyn is a scholar from Neverwinter
- **Freda** (gnome weaver): Redbrands avoid the Miner's Exchange (Halia Thornton)
- **Lanar** (human miner): Marauders attacking caravans on Triboar Trail, goblins involved
- **Narth** (human farmer): Sister Garaele returned wounded from a trip
- **Pip** (Toblen's son): Carp found secret tunnel, saw weird goblins with glowing hands

### Barthen's Provisions
**Proprietor**: Elmina Barthen (young human woman)
- **Delivery Reward**: 10 gp per character for wagon delivery
- **Information**: Gundren's brothers Nundro and Tharden camping outside town

**Resident**: Daran Edermath (500+ year old drow, retired adventurer)

- **Concerns**: Wants heroes to deal with Redbrands and "Glasstaff"

### Lionshield Coster

## Encounters in Phandalin

### Finding Cragmaw Castle
Most townsfolk don't know exact location, but:
- **Qelline Alderleaf**: Suggests asking druid Reidoth at Thundertree
- **Sildar Hallwinter**: Suggests searching for raiding parties

### Strange Goblins
Several NPCs mention odd encounters with goblins having elongated skulls and glowing green energy weapons (foreshadowing psionic goblins).

## Redbrand Ruffians Encounter

This confrontation can happen in several ways:
1. **Characters confront Redbrands** at Sleeping Giant
2. **Characters investigate** Tresendar Manor
3. **Redbrands seek out characters** if they're asking too many questions

### At the Sleeping Giant
> The Sleeping Giant is a ramshackle taproom at the east end of town. Several disheveled, surly humans linger on the covered porch, perched on empty ale barrels or leaning against the wall. They all wear grimy scarlet cloaks. Their stares are fixed on you as you approach.
>
> One of the ruffians spits on the ground. "Well, well," he says. "Here's a whole pack of little puppies. What do you want here, puppies? Come down here to bark at us?"

### Street Confrontation
> As you head into the street, you find several armed, surly humans waiting for you. Their scarlet cloaks are grimy, and they place their hands on their weapons as they watch you.
>
> One of the ruffians spits on the ground. "You've been asking too many questions," he snarls. "Time to move on, strangers. Give us your stuff and be on your way."

**Combat**: 4 Redbrand ruffians
- Not overly brave; remaining ruffian flees to Tresendar Manor if 3 defeated
- Can be captured/charmed for information

### What the Redbrands Know
- **Glasstaff**: Human wizard leader with glass staff, chambers in western stronghold
- **The Spider**: Mysterious figure who hired Redbrands, sent bugbears for muscle
- **Complex Guardian**: Hideous "eye monster" guards lower complex
- **Prisoners**: Captives held near old crypts, guarded by skeletons

## Redbrands' Hideout

### General Features
- **Structure**: Well-built chambers, flagstone floors, dressed stone walls
- **Layout**: Western end lower than eastern, stairs leading down
- **Ceilings**: 10 feet high unless noted
- **Doors**: Wood with iron fittings, locked doors DC 15 to pick/DC 20 to break
- **Secret Doors**: DC 10 Perception to find (1 minute search)
- **Light**: Oil lamps in wall sconces (most areas)

### Key Locations

#### R1: Cellar (Main Entrance)
- Landing 15 feet above cellar floor
- Stone cistern with fresh water (10 feet deep)
- **Hidden Treasure**: Waterproof satchel in cistern (Glasstaff's emergency kit)
- Barrels of provisions (salted meat, flour, ale)

#### R2: Barracks
- 3 human bandits (Redbrands)
- Double bunks, provisions
- **Treasure**: Total 43 sp, 17 gp, 2 garnets (10 gp each)

#### R3: Trapped Hall
- Hidden pit trap in center (DC 15 Perception, DC 15 Dex save)
- 20 feet deep, 2d6 damage, prone condition
- Copper-plated double door to crypts

#### R4: Tresendar Crypts
- 6 animated skeletons guard doors to R5/R6
- Password "Illefarn" or Redbrand cloak prevents attack
- 3 stone sarcophagi with carved lids
- **Treasure**: Jeweled gold bracelet (140 gp)

#### R5: Holding Cells
- 3 Redbrand ruffians as guards
- **Prisoners**: Mirna Dendrar and children (Nars 13, Nilsa 18)
- Cell doors: DC 10 to pick, DC 22 Strength to break
- **Mirna's Quest**: Emerald necklace hidden in Thundertree

#### R6: Armory
- Weapons and armor stockpile
- 12 red cloaks
- **Treasure**: 3 masterwork weapons (shortsword with gems, 2 silver-inlaid crossbows)

#### R7: Storeroom and Work Area
- Natural cavern with finished walls
- **Treasure**: 40 beaver pelts (2 gp each)
- Connects to southern cave exit

#### R8: Crevasse Cave
- **Guardian**: Nothic named Ssarnak
- 2 wooden bridges span 20-foot crevasse
- Southern bridge rigged to collapse (DC 10 Perception)
- **Dead Goblins**: 2 psionic goblins killed by nothic
- **Thel's Body**: Skeleton in crevasse with wooden unicorn pendant
- **Treasure**: Chest with coins, gems, potions, +1 longsword "Talon"

#### R9: Guard Barracks
- 4 bugbears, 1 goblin (Droop)
- Bugbears work for the Spider, know Wave Echo Cave location
- **Droop**: Frightened Scraptop goblin, not a threat
- **Treasure**: Nosk has 13 sp, gems (50 gp), master key

#### R10: Common Room
- 2 Redbrand ruffians, 2 human bandits playing knucklebones
- All intoxicated, loaded dice
- **Treasure**: 167 cp, 77 sp, 22 ep, 45 gp, ruby earring (30 gp)

#### R11: Wizard's Workshop
- **Guardian**: Quasit named Zeond (invisible, evil familiar)
- Alchemical equipment brewing potion of invisibility
- **Books**: Dwarvish journal about Phandelver Pact and Lightbringer mace
- **Treasure**: 3 rare reagents (25 gp each)

#### R12: Glasstaff's Quarters
- **Iarno "Glasstaff" Albrek**: Human mage with staff of defense
- Former Lords' Alliance member, now works for the Spider
- **Escape Route**: Secret door to R7 if warned by quasit
- **Letter from Spider**: Orders to capture/kill arriving strangers
- **Treasure**: 310 sp, 130 gp, gems, 2 spell scrolls

## Important Information from Glasstaff
- **The Spider**: A drow seeking the Forge of Spells
- **Bugbears**: Know route to Wave Echo Cave
- **Magical Forge**: Used by dwarves/gnomes to craft magic items
- **Lords' Alliance**: None know of his betrayal

## Faction Opportunities
Characters can join factions based on their actions:
- **Order of the Gauntlet**: Through Daran (honor, vigilance, justice)
- **Harpers**: Through Sister Garaele (equality, oppose abuse of power)
- **Lords' Alliance**: Through Sildar (protect communities)
- **Zhentarim**: Through Halia (wealth, power, influence)
- **Emerald Enclave**: Through connection to Reidoth (nature, balance)

## What's Next?
With the Redbrands defeated, characters should seek Wave Echo Cave. They need to:
- Find the cave's location (multiple leads point to wilderness)
- Consider faction quests from townspeople
- Prepare for extended wilderness travel
