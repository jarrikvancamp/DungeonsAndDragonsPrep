# Daran Edermath

## Role
Retired drow adventurer and orchard keeper, Order of the Gauntlet contact

## Hook
Wise former adventurer who understands the burdens of heroic life and offers guidance.

## Motivation
Protect Phandalin from threats while enjoying peaceful retirement among his apple trees.

## Appearance
- Distinguished drow with silver-streaked white hair and kind purple eyes
- Weathered hands speak of both sword work and honest farming labor
- Wears simple farmer's clothes but moves with unconscious warrior's grace

## Personality
- **Traits**: Patient mentor who speaks thoughtfully; has seen much of the world's darkness and light
- **Bond**: Believes new heroes must rise to face coming threats
- **Flaw**: Sometimes expects too much of younger adventurers too quickly

## Quest Hooks
- Wants heroes to investigate troubles at Old Owl Well
- Can provide Order of the Gauntlet missions
- Offers tactical advice for dealing with Redbrands

## Key Information
- Knows Redbrands operate from Tresendar Manor cellars
- Has fought similar threats during his adventuring days
- Understands the importance of the Phandelver Pact's legacy
- Recognizes signs of larger threats emerging in the region

## Order of the Gauntlet Mission: Old Owl Well
- **Problem**: Undead spotted at old watchtower ruins
- **Concern**: Necromantic activity threatens trade routes
- **Reward**: Boots of Striding and Springing if threat eliminated
- **Intelligence**: Can provide map and historical details

## Services Offered
- **Tactical Advice**: Combat strategies based on 500+ years experience
- **Equipment Maintenance**: Can repair weapons and armor
- **Regional Knowledge**: Information about historical sites and threats
- **Safe Haven**: Orchard provides secure meeting place

## Lines to Speak
1. "In my centuries of life, I've learned that evil thrives when good folk choose to look away. You've chosen to act."
2. "The Redbrands think their manor cellars are secret, but I've been watching their movements for months."
3. "Old Owl Well holds dangerous memories. If undead walk there again, it threatens more than just travelers."
