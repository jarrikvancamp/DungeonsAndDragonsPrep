# Animated Skeletons

## Role
Undead guardians of the Tresendar family crypts

## Tactical Overview
**Combat Style**: Relentless undead warriors with shortswords and shields
**Preferred Range**: Melee formation fighting
**Key Abilities**: Undead immunities, pack coordination, persistent threat

## Stats
```yaml
Skeleton:
  AC: 13 (Armor Scraps, Shield)
  HP: 13 (range 10-16)
  Speed: 30 ft
  STR: 10 DEX: 14 CON: 15 INT: 6 WIS: 8 CHA: 5
  
  Damage Vulnerabilities: Bludgeoning
  Damage Immunities: Poison
  Condition Immunities: Exhaustion, poisoned
  Languages: Understands Common but can't speak
  Challenge: 1/4 (50 XP)
  
  Equipment: Rusty shortsword, rotted shield, tattered armor scraps
  
  Actions:
    Shortsword: +4 to hit, 1d6+2 slashing damage
    Shield Bash: +2 to hit, 1d4 bludgeoning damage (opportunity attacks)
```

## Encounter Details (R4: Tresendar Crypts)

### Numbers & Positioning
- **6 Animated Skeletons**: Guard three stone sarcophagi
- **Formation**: 2 skeletons per sarcophagus, flanking formation
- **Activation**: Attack anyone entering without proper authorization

### Bypass Methods
- **Password "Illefarn"**: Ancient family name prevents attack
- **Redbrand Cloak**: Wearing red cloak grants safe passage
- **Stealth**: DC 15 to sneak past without triggering (very difficult)
- **Turning Undead**: Cleric abilities work normally

## Tactics
**Activation**: All 6 animate simultaneously when trigger conditions met
**Formation**: Spread out to prevent area effect spells
**Persistence**: Fight until destroyed, no morale checks
**Coordination**: Flank enemies and protect each other

## Historical Context
- **Tresendar Family**: Ancient noble family that built the manor
- **Burial Site**: Legitimate family crypt with ceremonial remains
- **Magic Ward**: Animated by protective enchantments, not necromancy
- **Password**: "Illefarn" was the Tresendar family's ancient homeland

## Sarcophagi Details
- **Stone Construction**: Heavy carved lids, family crests
- **Contents**: Ancient bones, rotted burial goods
- **No Treasure**: Family valuables long since removed or decayed
- **Respect**: Disturbing remains might anger other undead

## Combat Environment
- **Copper Doors**: Ornate double doors from trapped hall (R3)
- **Stone Floor**: Good footing, no environmental hazards
- **Cramped Space**: 20x30 foot room limits movement
- **Multiple Exits**: Doors to R5 and R6 (armory and cells)

## Undead Characteristics
- **Ancient**: Bones show centuries of age
- **Clothing**: Tattered remnants of once-fine garments
- **Weapons**: Corroded but still functional
- **Eyes**: Empty sockets with faint blue glow

## Tactical Considerations
- **Vulnerability**: Bludgeoning weapons deal double damage
- **Immunities**: Poison effects completely useless
- **Area Effects**: Grouped formation vulnerable to *Burning Hands*, etc.
- **Turning**: Cleric abilities can control or destroy multiple skeletons

## Development Options
- **Peaceful Bypass**: Password or cloak allows exploration without combat
- **Partial Engagement**: Possible to trigger only some skeletons
- **Reinforcements**: Don't summon help (isolated encounter)
- **Aftermath**: Bodies crumble to dust when destroyed

## Treasure
- **Individual**: No treasure per skeleton
- **Sarcophagi**: Jeweled gold bracelet (140 gp) hidden in one tomb
- **Equipment**: Weapons too corroded to be valuable
- **Historical**: Family records might interest scholars

## Scaling Options
**Easier**: Reduce to 4 skeletons, HP 10 each
**Harder**: Add 2 more skeletons, some have bows for ranged attacks
**Environmental**: Add difficult terrain from broken stone
**Tactical**: Skeletons use better formation fighting and shield walls
